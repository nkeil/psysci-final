Background

In Mexico, even before the arrival of the Spaniards in the sixteenth century, mushrooms had an important ceremonial and sacred importance. In Mexican culture, mushrooms began being consumed around 13,000 years ago in the first groups of hunter-gatherers. By the 16th century, the knowledge of wild mushrooms was widespread, diverse, and extremely detailed. Over 5,000 common names are known for edible wild fungi in different Mexican languages.

The story shown on this codex links to the conception of the first appearance of the sun and its relationship with mushrooms. It corresponds to the creation of the universe as we know it, and depicts rites of corn and pulque (a sacred beverage in Mesoamerica). This pictographic document was written on a long screen of danned deer hide treated with layers of stucco, a material that allowed the hide to be drawn on. It has 52 sheets of size approximately 22cm x 26cm painted on both sides.
